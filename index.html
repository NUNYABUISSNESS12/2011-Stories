The Magic Guy Made By Bentley

I woke up and immediately thought, "Where am I?" This wasn't my home. Then, I saw a man who said, "I will give you superpowers."

I screamed, "I don't care! Let me go!" The man clicked some buttons on a strange device, and suddenly, I was transported home. I woke up in a familiar place, but it seemed somehow different. But what was different?

Suddenly, a scientist appeared and said, “Let's run some tests.” Suddenly, I blacked out, and I woke up in an operating room in a bed and for some reason I couldn't move. I yelled, “What's going on!?”

The strange scientist appeared, and he had a syringe with a green ooze. All of a sudden, he gave me a shot from the syringe, and I suddenly found myself in a hospital room, and some doctors said, “Oh thank God you’re okay; we thought we lost you.” I replied, “What happened to me?”

One of the doctors said, “You have been in a coma for the past three weeks, and according to our research, a rare animal bit you. The animal doesn't have any data in our database.”

Another doctor asked, “Are you okay?”

I shook my head. “I don't remember what happened; I had the best memory.”

The first doctor said, “You’re being discharged; please call us at 800-555-HELP if anything comes up.”

I got up and stretched my arms. My hand pointed at a box of tissues; it started to float. I screamed, “I have Telekinesis!”

The doctors gasped in amazement, “You have superpowers! Wow, if that's a side effect we’ll have to keep a super close eye on you.”

I walked toward the door, tried to close it, and reached for the handle. When I pulled the handle, it suddenly snapped off. “YOU HAVE SUPERSTRENGTH?”, they exclaimed. I then apologized for breaking their door, and they asked, "Can we get an autograph?"

The I.T. Guy

Chapter 12

I was in my superhero headquarters when suddenly, a message flashed on the main computer screen, displaying an urgent warning: "HACKED!! Base is on lockdown!"

Instantly, all the digital clocks switched to a special mode, their screens now reading: "Lockdown!"

My phone rang, and Zack’s voice came through the automated message: "Hey, it's Zack. This is an automated message. If you're hearing this, the building is on lockdown, and all exits are sealed. If you're in your office, rewire the clock 24 hours forward, and the lockdown will lift. If you're not in your office... well, you're out of luck."

The call ended. I rushed to my office door, but it was locked. Thinking fast, I grabbed a coat hanger and straightened it out. Carefully, I slid it under the door, hooked it around the handle, and pulled. The door clicked open.

Glancing around, I searched for a clock. I found one hidden inside a cabinet. Wasting no time, I rewired it to move forward by 24 hours.

Suddenly, all the clocks in the building flashed: "Lockdown Over!"

The Science Lab

By Bentley Playz

Chapter 1: The Beginning

It was a bright Tuesday morning, and the sun was shining. I was in my lab, hidden inside my house through a secret entrance. Suddenly, one of the scientists in the lab spoke to me — the guy with the tag that said “BOB.” Bob said, “Hey— boss we made a device that can open a portal.” I asked, “What The Portal To? Where does it go?” Bob told me, “According to the infinite universe paradox there are infinite worlds and we found a gate into it.” I responded to Bob, “Bob what does this mean?” Bob replied, “Well, we can go to the universe that has SpongeBob, or an opposite universe, or the ‘The Magic Football’ Universe.” I replied, “How is this possible?” Another scientist called “Gregory” came up and Greg said, “Boss the portal is taking too much power—it’s going to blow.” I quickly replied, “Bob, shut down the portal now.” Bob immediately went into action, typing furiously. He said, “I can shut it down, but we’re going to blackout the city.” Greg replied, “Let me check—we got 60 seconds. Bob, shut it down or we’re dead.” I replied, “Bob, move out of the way—I have the shutdown code.” I immediately went to the computer and clicked “EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN.” The computer said “Enter code,” and then Greg interrupted, “Quick—20 seconds.” I immediately typed 0012847 then ENTER, and then a popup appeared: “PORTAL SHUTDOWN.” Suddenly an emergency broadcast came on the monitor after a minute when the power came back on: “This is Sarah with the 5 o’clock news. Live power outage after a huge amount of power was being taken into an unknown location. The FBI is on the line tracing the location as we speak.” I immediately panicked and went on the intercom: “Attention everyone, at this time the lab is being shut down until further notice. Thank you — Bentley.”

END OF CHAPTER 1

Chapter 2: FBI Investigation

I went to my kitchen to make macaroni and cheese and heard a knock: “FBI, open up.” I opened the door and saw ten officers and a news team. I asked, “May I help you?” The FBI agent replied softly, “I have reason to believe you caused a blackout. Do you mind if we search?” I replied, “Do you have a warrant?” They said, “Yes, we have a search warrant.” I replied, “Alright then, feel free to search.” Their team searched every nook and cranny—the bedroom, the bathroom, the kitchen, my bedroom. Then the agents came back to the front. “Alright, so sorry to waste your time.” Then the team and the news left.

END OF CHAPTER 2

Chapter 3: Lab Recovery

I went back to my lab after calling the scientists down. I found Bob and asked him, “Is there a way we can stabilize the portal? We used way too much power and almost got caught.” Bob replied softly, “Yeah, I can attempt to edit the power usage or use a generator — which would you like me to do?” I replied, “Let’s use a generator to stabilize the power usage. Also, is the portal even safe?” Fred, the electronics guy, came to fix the power usage. He brought a soldering gun and soldered a new electronic power stabilizer. Then he said, “Yeah, the portal is as stabilized as possible—you’re all clear to use it.”

END OF CHAPTER 3

Chapter 4: The Portal

I turned on the portal and set it to the “Gold World.” Bob said, “Wait!”—then he had a flashback, remembering that his pet dog had been kidnapped by someone who opened a portal using a device and disappeared. Bob said, “Here, take this radio in case the portal closes. The radio has advanced technology that makes it work in any universe.” I took and equipped the radio and then hopped into the portal. Suddenly, the entire world was gold. I piled gold into my bag and found my house—it was the same but richer. After an hour, the radio went off: “You got 2 minutes—the portal is getting unstable. I may not be able to reopen the portal. Hello, do you copy?” I replied, “I have traveled quite a bit—I’m unsure where the portal is.” I received an instant reply, “Click the find button on the radio, then click portal.” The radio spoke, “Head north 20 feet, then head west 5 feet.” I followed the directions, found the golden portal, and hopped back in. Bob said, “Alright, the portal needs to recharge for about an hour.” I asked, “So why did you make this—what’s the point?” Bob replied sadly, “My dog. They took my dog—someone using a portal took my dog…” I replied, “We will find them and bring your dog back at all cost.”

END OF CHAPTER 4

Chapter 5: Bob’s Dog

I asked the greatest scientist ever, “Jerry,” “How can we trace which universe took Bob’s dog?” Jerry replied, “Do we have DNA from the dog? I can make an add-on to the portal that searches the universes to find the dog.” Jerry started working—building a metal box, adding wiring and supports to the portal. Two hours later, Jerry finished the add-on and plugged it in. I found Bob and asked for DNA. He gave me a piece of hair, which I placed in the tray. After 20 minutes, the locator found a universe—the “Criminal Verse.” I got equipment from Bob. He warned, “It may be dangerous. I have equipment for this mission.” He handed me an upgraded radio with gravity control, backup calls, and equipment drop-offs. Also, he gave me an invulnerable sword. I hesitated; we only had 1 hour and 2 minutes. Jerry came and made a dial add-on to extend time and shut down the portal. “Alright, you’re all set,” Jerry said.

END OF CHAPTER 5

Chapter 6: Recovering the Dog

I hopped into the portal and saw a disturbing old world—a worn-down road. A local said, “Who the heck are you? I ain’t ever seen you around here. Where you come from?” I replied, “I’m not looking for trouble—I’m new around here.” I walked away and went on the radio, “Beep boop… Hello, does anyone copy?” I heard alien-like replies and realized I was on the wrong channel. I switched and repeated my question. Jerry replied, “Hit the GPS button—it’ll lead you there.” I hit the button labeled “GPS.” It said, “Head left 2 miles, then go forward 1.5 miles.” I followed the instructions and reached a sketchy house, abandoned for 20 years with broken windows. I knocked, and someone answered: “Hello, how can I help you?” I replied, “I’m from a different universe—I came to retrieve my friend’s dog.” The guy said, “You mean Fido? I got him 3 years ago from an Earth dimension. I thought he was mine—sorry, take your dog back.” I took the dog and returned to give him back to Bob.

AND EVERYONE LIVED HAPPILY EVER AFTER. THE END.
